Gacha User Flag Analysis — HC-Pack Dominant Early Whales · D30
Base: Early whales where HC_Pack ≥50% of D30 revenue (255 users total — 64 gacha=0, 159 gacha=1). gacha_flag=1 = spent ≥50% of those hitcoins on gacha pulls (SPB_10X / GACHA_BOX). gacha_flag=0 = HC deployed into upgrades, equipment, elite deals etc. Both groups are identical in purchase behaviour — the flag captures only what they do with HC after buying it.
Pity context: WC character pity is 75 pulls with a 1% lucky chance per pull before pity hits. gacha=1 median pulls at D30 = 13, top decile = 62 — meaning even the heaviest pullers in this cohort haven't hit pity. Only 38/159 gacha=1 users got a WC character (24%), mostly via lucky pulls. Most are spending HC on an incomplete loop — committed to gacha but not deep enough to complete the pity cycle within 30 days.

The incomplete loop problem: gacha=1 users are spending HC on pulls but 76% (121/159) are not getting a WC character by D30. The median gacha=1 user has done 13 pulls — they need 62 more to hit pity. At current HC spend velocity, most will churn before completing the cycle. This is a retention risk embedded in the gacha mechanic itself for early whales: enough engagement to spend, not enough to be rewarded.
gacha=0 users show what healthy HC deployment looks like: fewer pulls (median 8.5), HC redirected into upgrades and equipment, 1.8× higher ARPPU, 9pp better D30 retention. These users found a progression loop that keeps them in the game. The product question is whether gacha=1 users can be nudged toward similar behaviour — either by making the pity cycle achievable within D30, or by surfacing upgrade/equipment sinks more prominently to HC buyers.
