Score vs Win Rate Correlation · By Tier
Framework validated. Overall score (batting + bowling) correlates strongly with win rate across all tiers (r=0.597, p=0.0000, n=1,410). Higher scores consistently predict higher win rates. Both batting and bowling contribute independently — overall score is the strongest single predictor.

How scores are calculated

Each early whale user is assigned a batting score (0–15) and a bowling score (0–15) based on their over-level performance during the week of Jun 17–23, benchmarked against winning innings data for their tier.

For batting, we look at each user's median runs scored at each of the 5 overs. Each over is independently categorised against the win==1 benchmark for that tier — Elite (above win q75) scores 3, Good (win q50–q75) scores 2, Needs Work (win q25–q50) scores 1, Bad (below win q25) scores 0. Summing across all 5 overs gives a batting score from 0 to 15.

Bowling follows the same structure but with inverted logic since lower runs conceded is better — Elite (below win q25) scores 3, Good (win q25–q50) scores 2, Needs Work (win q50–q75) scores 1, Bad (above win q75) scores 0. Summing across 5 overs gives a bowling score from 0 to 15.

The overall score (0–30) is the sum of batting and bowling scores. All benchmarks are tier-specific, derived from win==1 over-level data, so a user is always compared against what good performance looks like at their own tier. Win rate is calculated only from matches played at the user's highest tier during the week. The framework is validated — overall score correlates with win rate at r=0.597 (p=0.0000) across 1,410 users, with tier-level correlations ranging from r=0.772 (SUPREME) to r=0.970 (TITAN).

Key reads: REMARKABLE has the strongest bowling correlation (r=0.977). LEGENDARY and TITAN have the strongest batting correlation (r=0.981, r=0.954). TITAN bowling is the weakest predictor (r=0.485, p=0.19) — suggesting at TITAN level, bowling variance is less predictive than batting quality. MASTERFUL and SUPREME show the lowest absolute win rates — consistent with those being the most competitive mid-tiers.
