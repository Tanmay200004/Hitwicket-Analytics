Runs & Wickets Split by Match Outcome · All Tiers
Runs gap between wins and losses is large and consistent across all tiers. Winners score 6–15 more runs per over at median vs losers. The gap widens at q90 — winners score 77–89 at the top decile vs 61–68 for losers. TITAN shows the largest absolute gap (q50: 64 win vs 49 loss = 15 runs).
Wickets tell a sharper story in losses. In wins, q50 wickets = 1–2. In losses, q50 jumps to 2–3 and q90 reaches 4–5. Losing innings are characterised by both fewer runs AND more wickets — a double signal.
Over-level pattern (see Over-Level Benchmark tab): Over 2 is universally the highest-scoring over across all tiers in winning innings. Over 3 is the universal weak point — every tier's q50 dips back to 12 at over 3 even in wins. TITAN and LEGENDARY accelerate in overs 4–5; most other tiers flatten. Losing innings show a sharper over 3 dip and fail to recover in overs 4–5.

Runs Category — Elite / Good / Needs Work / Bad · vs Win Benchmark · Per Tier × Over
Benchmarks are tier-specific, based on win==1 data. Elite = above win q75 · Good = win q50 to q75 · Needs Work = win q25 to q50 · Bad = below win q25. "Bad" now means below what even the bottom quartile of winning innings looks like for that tier — a meaningful floor. Most users sit in Needs Work, confirming the benchmark is calibrated correctly.
Over 5 and Over 1 are consistently the weakest overs across tiers — Bad counts peak at these overs. Over 4 has the fewest Bad users in most tiers. TITAN has zero Elite users at overs 1, 2 and 5 — the higher benchmark makes Elite harder to achieve even for the top tier.
