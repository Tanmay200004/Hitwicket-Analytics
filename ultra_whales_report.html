Observed Behavioural Archetypes
Archetype A — Upgrade Velocity Whales (6a0d0a60): Revenue dominated by SUT + HC_Pack. HC goes primarily into PURCHASE_ENHANCER, EQUIPMENT_PURCHASE, PASSIVE_TRAIT_LEVEL_UP. Low IN_GAME_CURRENCY_CONVERSION. These users are actively deploying HC into upgrade mechanics, not accumulating or converting. High txn frequency (156). Intent signal is clear: they want to push player strength faster.
Archetype B — HC Bridge Users (6a111d54, 69fa2501, 6a12e104): High HC_Pack revenue share (46–67%). 44–69% of HC spend is IN_GAME_CURRENCY_CONVERSION. These users are buying HC at scale and converting it into another resource — likely SUT. They are constrained by SUT availability and using HC as a bypass. This is a significant latent demand signal: if SUT purchase paths were improved or HC→SUT conversion were more efficient, these users could spend more.
Archetype C — Character Acquisition Whales (6a09f226, 6a0097398, 69d1213a): Top HC sink is SPB_10X_OPEN (71–92%). These users are laser-focused on building roster depth via shard pulls. HC buying is driven by character collection, not progression. Revenue mix skews toward cash purchases for characters (Others, VIP_OFFER_TREE) alongside HC_Pack for pulls. Offer cadence and character release frequency are the key lever for this segment.
Archetype D — Fully Activated Whale (69c53bdd): Highest revenue (₹5.6L) and HC spend (5.26L HC). HC sinks are distributed across SPB_10X (30%), PURCHASED_ELITE_DEALS (20%), EQUIPMENT_PURCHASE (15%), BOUGHT_INGAME_ITEM + PLAYER_UPGRADE (10% each), PURCHASE_ENHANCER (10%). This user is engaging with every system simultaneously. The benchmark archetype — what full activation looks like.
Observation — IN_GAME_CURRENCY_CONVERSION as a bottleneck signal: The 3 bridge-currency users collectively represent ₹4.9L in revenue and are routing 44–69% of HC through a conversion step. If this conversion is a SUT bypass, it confirms the existing finding that SUT scarcity is the binding constraint for upgrade-velocity users. These users' spend curves may be artificially suppressed by conversion inefficiency rather than low intent.
